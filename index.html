Project 2 Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Boning Yang — boyang@ucsd.edu

Proposition: The participation of women in higher education has improved worldwide significantly over the past decade.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Upward-sloping line with blue-green gradient evokes sense of steady progress. (+1) – Persuasive but not misleading.
  • Title wording “steadily” minimizes post-2019 decline. (–1) – Slight rhetorical framing bias.
  • Full y-axis (0–55%) maintains proportional honesty. (+2) – Transparent visualization.

Data transformation: Aggregated world-level averages (2010–2020) from World Bank data; smoothed annual noise for clarity.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Truncated x-axis from 30–100% magnifies inequality gap. (–2) – Deliberate exaggeration for rhetorical effect.
  • Red-green color contrast implies moral judgment. (–1) – Emotionally charged framing.
  • Ordered bars by income group to emphasize inequality. (+1) – Persuasive yet transparent ordering.

Data transformation: Averaged female enrollment by World Bank income level; applied axis truncation to heighten visual contrast.

Final Reflection

The “for” visualization presents the global upward trend in female tertiary enrollment between 2010 and 2020. Its blue-green gradient reinforces the sense of progress, though the title “steadily” and color blending slightly downplay the post-2019 decline. The full y-axis keeps proportional integrity.

The “against” visualization, by contrast, emphasizes inequality between developed and developing nations. Its truncated x-axis (30–100%) and red-green palette visually amplify disparity, using emotional framing to suggest that education equality is still a privilege of rich nations.

From my perspective, although global averages hide large inequalities, the data clearly show that women’s participation in higher education has increased substantially in the past decade. Thus, I lean toward the proposition while acknowledging the rhetorical power of design.
